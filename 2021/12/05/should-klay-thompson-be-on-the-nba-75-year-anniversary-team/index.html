Should Klay Thompson be on the NBA 75 year anniversary team?

2021-12-05

To commemorate the 75 year anniversary of one of the most popular professional sports leagues in the world, the NBA released their 75 year anniversary team. Like the previous two additions of the list that come every 25 years, the list was filled with names founding fathers of the NBA, players who ran the league during it’s boom in the 80s and 90s, and even current players. But when trying to limit a leagues history to 75 players, there’s always controversy.

Klay Thompson spearheaded some of the complaints. For the days follow the release of the list, Thompson caused a stir by claiming he should have been on the list. Today, I’m going to try and answer that question. Should Klay Thompson be on the list of top 75 NBA players ever?

As you can see, Klay is below average in all three major categories: not a great look for his place on this list. It’s worth noting that he’s still an active player. So now, let’s compare him to other active players and see how he’s done relative to them.

We’ll start by sorting active players.

data %>%
  filter(active == "yes") -> active

I want to add something a little less quantitative in here: accolades. We’ll figure that by adding All-NBA selection and All Star selections. For my chart, I also want to know if the player has won any MVP’s , so I’ll throw that in there as well.

active2 <- active %>%
  group_by(player,position) %>%
  mutate(accolades = allstar+allnba) %>%
  summarize(
    TotalPoints = sum(totalpoints),
    rings = sum(rings),
    MVP = sum(mvp),
    Accolades = sum(accolades)
  )
## `summarise()` has grouped output by 'player'. You can override using the `.groups` argument.

MVPStatus <- active2 %>%
