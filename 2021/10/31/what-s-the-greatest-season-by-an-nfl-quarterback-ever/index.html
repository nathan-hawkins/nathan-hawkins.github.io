What's the greatest season by an NFL quarterback ever?

2021-10-31

What I witnessed while watching the Green Bay Packers on Sundays in 2020 was truly special. My team, which I can say because technically I’m an owner of the Packers, finished the season 13-3. Top 10 defense, a handful of All-Pros, and a coach with some swagger that brought excitement to our offense. Above all of that, I watched quarterback Aaron Rodgers dice up the field he was Gordon Ramsay in Hell’s Kitchen.

Playing for the team who just recently drafted his replacement, Rodgers had 48 touchdowns and just 5 interceptions on his way to the third MVP season of his career. When the season was over, I had to ask the question: Was this the greatest season a quarterback has ever played?

That leads us to this project. I’ve gathered in-depth data dating back to 1967, the first season of the Super Bowl. In total, there are 275 individual seasons that I felt were worth pondering further as a potential greatest season of all-time.

The critera wasn’t too convuluted: if you passed for at least 26 touchdowns in a season, welcome to the list! Every player to pass for at least 26 touchdowns is part of the player pool, and there were a few other exceptions who I put on the list as I thought they might be notable. The inital stats I took from each players season were: Passing Touchdowns, team wins, losses and ties, passer rating, passing yards, passing yards per game, interceptions, pass completions and pass attempts. I also made note if that player won a conference championship and/or Super Bowl in the season in which they’re listed. The process was tedious, but it ultimately gave me a comprehensive look at some of the best seasons by a quarterback in league history.

For the first time, lets mutate in our own data. The percent of passes that were touchdowns, and the percent that were interceptions.

eliteness %>%
  mutate(intpercent = (Int/Passattempts)*100) %>% arrange(desc(intpercent)) -> eliteness

eliteness %>%
mutate(tdpercent = (TD/Passattempts)*100) %>% arrange(desc(tdpercent)) -> eliteness

A good way to tell quarterback quality is touchdown percentage to interception percentage. So why don’t we try finding the difference between the two. The larger the number, the better the season. Since we’re finding the best of the best, lets call it elite score.

eliteness %>%
  mutate(elitescore = (tdpercent - intpercent)) %>% arrange(desc(elitescore)) -> eliteness

To display this data, I’m going to make a table. Elite Score alone is cool, but we should add some other stats to give a bit of context. Lets add in TD% and INT%, which were the two datasets we used to come up with Elite Score. We’ll also add in passing yards, touchdowns, and interceptions, as they are all good general overviews of player success. Lastly, the top ten is elitescore of more than 6.33. Lets filter those players in.

eliteness %>%
